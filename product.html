We live in an uncertain world, and there are many threats out there that might disrupt your company’s ability to do business. To help mitigate these threats, companies create business continuity plans.

A business continuity plan is a strategy to get a business up and running as soon as possible in the event of some type of disruption. The first step of creating a business continuity plan is conducting a risk assessment. In a risk assessment, you try to think of all possible business continuity threats and attempt to devise a plan to get the business up and running again as soon as possible should one of these threats occur.

Here are the top threats to business continuity faced by Finance and Banking companies today

Cyber attacks: are one of the most potent threats that businesses face. There were a number of high-profile businesses throughout the world that fell victim to cyber attacks in 2015. As a result, in 2016 cyber attacks was ranked as the number one threat to business continuity. Especially Finance and Banking institutions must have a plan to defend against and mitigate the effects of this threat.

Blackouts: Is your building secure in the event of a prolonged blackout? Ideally, you’ll have a generator on site to keep the company running, but are you prepared for a scenario in which the power is out for days. Cloud migration and Digitisation is something we implement to mitigate this risk.

Workplace Violence: Unfortunately, the threat of an active shooter situation has become a common fear in contemporary American society. Your employees must know exactly what you expect them to do in the event of an active shooter situation. Be sure to train your employees on what to do if a gunman enters the building. Also, stage active shooter drills so employees are familiar with the procedure. We aid to conduct such and more table top drills that increase employee engagement in the Business continuity Plans with is very important to the success of these strategies.
